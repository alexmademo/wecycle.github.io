The story

The world population has grown from 2 to 8 billion in the past 100 years. Cities are growing to accommodate this population. To maintain interconnectivity, cities have to continuously invent new mobility solutions.

In the 20th century, the primary mobility solution was the private automobile. The road infrastructure that resulted from this solution is a legacy we are living with today. Even in the densest centers of the most walkable cities with robust public trasport systems, the road infrastructure which privileges the car continues to dominate the built environment.
Barcelona
Shanghai

This infrastructure legacy has two critical impacts:
1. CO2 emissions. In the EU, automobiles account for 12% of CO2 emissions.
2. The loss of community cohesion. Owning a car results in longer distances between urban activities, and increased isolation from your neighbors.
New York City

A bicycle is a car without a motor.
A bicycle is a pedestrian with wheels.

Many mobility companies have already proposed solutions, namely those related to the sharing on-demand economy. (Uber/Jump, Mobike, Ofo). They aim to make bicycling more convenient, cheap, and flexible.

The problem is that they are TOO convenient, TOO cheap, TOO flexible. To maintain their bottom line, these companies are mass-producing bicycles and dropping them onto city streets, causing congestion and clutter on sidewalks. It fails to address CO2 emissions and the cohesion of communities.

Look at the numbers. Shared bikes only represent about 10% of the total bike stock out there. The rest are private bicycles, 50% of which sit unused. Instead of mass-producing new bicycles, why not take advantage of this unused resource?

This is the gap that WeCycle fills. It is an easy-to-use p2p platform which allows people to rent their unused bicycle to anyone who needs it. Users can log on, browse available bikes by location, type, and price, and contact the renter directly.

Individuals meet in person to complete the transaction, which builds trust. After the bicycle is returned, users of the platform rate and review each other. Like Craigslist and Airbnb, WeCycle considers face-to-face to be an important aspect of p2p.

OUR CORE PHILOSOPHIES:
1. Strengthen communities
2. Reduce CO2 emissions
3. Activate private bicycles
4. Create open bicycle mobility data
